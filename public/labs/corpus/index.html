the work

corpus, 2026

networked text, visitor-triggered web page

corpus is a live sentence written by the fact of being visited. When the page remains visible for two seconds, it adds one small unit of language to a shared body. There is nothing to type, click, or submit; attention is the only gesture the work accepts.

privacy

Each addition is recorded in an open event journal. To prevent accidental repeats, the system keeps only short hashed traces of the session and network address. It stores no raw addresses, full user agents, fingerprints, or analytics.

preservation

The page itself is the work. The journal records its history, and the static snapshot preserves one view of it for citation. Neither replaces the live surface.
